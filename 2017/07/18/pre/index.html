Securitization

1 Securitization Part

Thanks Shuba for giving us a big picture on reverse mortgage securitization. I will then add some colors on that, hope you guys like it.
I will briefly discuss the cash flow operation of the securitization systems.
Firstly, as the graph shows lenders and borrowers interact through the Trust, equivelently a fund.

Cash flow chart

  • Investors deposit an initial amount about 76 million dollar to the trust and then enjoys a monthly repayment of coupon and principal from the Fund Account according to the "Water-Fall" structure.

  • Borrowers receive amount "Funding Amount" from the Trust monthly, which u can regard this part as the monthly partial withdraw from the Manulife case.

  • if borrowers path away or moves out from the collateral home, Trustees can take over their assets and turn their property into part of fund (if the home price is higher than the collateral value, excess part will return to the elders if them still alive, or distribute to their relatives according to the distribution process of heritage by law; if the home value is less than the collateral value, investor may suffers in the case that all the homes are averagely depreciated).

  • the distribution process also present in the chart. every month, trustee will calculate a account called "Available Payment Account", which calculated via the formulae here.

  • APA is pretty easy to understand, it is merely the cash flow generate from the loans plus residuals of last period and repurchase from the elders minus the cash flows out to the trustees and account payable to investor last period and monthly payment to the elders.

  • Explain terminology: excess amount, additional account = interest short fall...

  • After a scheduled payment is done by APA, any money left go back to be a part of fund account.

SASCO is carefully designed and the designer keep it attractive by trench design (credit enhancement) and further with kind of high interest payments and coupons.

2 hedging part

Even through that securitization transfers a huge risk to a every individuals, and lower the overall risks. However, investors are still exposed to huge risks we introduced in the former ppts. Hence we come to the hedge part, how investors can hedge their position on reverse mortgage backed securities and soothe their future revenues?

  • interest instruments are instruments that the hedgers most commonly use.

    • ppt 4.1 interest gains hedge for the loss of house depreciation
    • pros and cons for using swaps and caps
    • it not work at systematical case

  • House Derivatives

    • House Derivatives directly trading on house price. Such as HPA futures, options and some swaps.
    • HPA futures, options traded upon The S&P/Case-Shiller index. Hence, put options on index is a good hedger.
    • Total return swaps on housing index and ABX.HE (index on subordinate debts) spread widen is atypical cases.

  • Reverse mortgage insurance policy

    • The insurance would indemnify against losses incurred when proceeds from the sale of the mortgaged property fail to satisfy the then-outstanding principal and interest.

  • Medical Underwriting

    • Medical underwriting is a health insurance term referring to the use of medical or health information in the evaluation of an applicant for coverage.
    • risk mainly comes from mispricing, if people measure the mortality in a more proper way, investors are under less risks.
